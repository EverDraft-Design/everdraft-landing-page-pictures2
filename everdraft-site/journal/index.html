FIRST ENTRY

The First Draft of EverDraft

Like many kids in the late 2000s, I found myself waiting impatiently for the next Harry Potter book to be released.

I had reread the books so many times, and while I was ready for something new, I still loved the world. That was when I discovered Harry Potter fan fiction — and you could say I said goodbye to the next few years of my life.

But I don’t see it that way.

What I found was endless stories set in a world I loved, with characters I adored thrown into new, strange, hilarious, heartbreaking, and sometimes very non-canon situations. There was always something new waiting for me to read.

And when the stories I found stopped quite hitting the mark, I started writing my own.

Reviews and feedback kept me tapping away at my keyboard. Those readers will forever be in my heart — the ones who encouraged me, laughed at my plot devices, and celebrated when a story reached its natural end.

I loved the community of it all.

Readers left uplifting reviews on my ramblings. Graphic designers would happily whip up story banners and send over an image link as a gift. Beta readers gave their time and thoughts in exchange for getting to see a fresh chapter first.

It felt creative, generous, and alive.

Of course, I eventually grew out of fan fiction — though we’re certainly not going to say no to it here at EverDraft — but my love for reading and writing never dimmed.

Now, the personal problem at hand: it has been decades since anyone has read my writing.

And frankly, I miss it.

I know I could post on similar sites and hope for the best. I could share my work with my best friend, but the thought of watching her face change as she reads something that came straight from my soul makes me squirm, no matter how much I trust her.

The internet, for all its chaos, is full of people with different interests, skills, passions, and perspectives. I want to find them.

I want to find the readers who want to say, I read this draft first.

I want to find the writers who are looking for their tribe — people who will build them up, cheer them on, and help them keep going when the doubt creeps in.

I want to fully immerse myself in that world again.

And with that in mind, I created EverDraft.

I envision EverDraft as a positive, encouraging community of like-minded souls hoping to grow their creativity one draft at a time. A space for readers, writers, beta readers, editors, graphic designers, and everyone who loves being part of the storytelling process.

So, help me get this idea off the ground.

Sick of posting your stories only to have them buried?

That’s not likely to happen here — at least not for now.

I invite you to join the waitlist and come along on this whirlwind adventure with me.

— Carley
